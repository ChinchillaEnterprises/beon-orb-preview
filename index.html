BeonIQ · Beon Orb Animation Preview
v1.1 · Same designs, slower speeds per Marta + Peter feedback. Pulse 5s · Breathe 5s · Ring 10s · Morph 10s.
Replacing the existing Tuck logo with a new Beon orb per marketing direction. Each video below shows the orb in two states side by side: the default state (black orb with yellow letter, used at idle) and the active state (yellow orb with black letter, used during interaction or when actively listening). The "b" letterform is approximated with Oswald 500 in this v1 — we will tighten alignment with the official SVG when marketing provides it.
Option 1
Halo Pulse
Glow alternates intensity at a 5.0 second cycle
Subtle, idle-friendly. The yellow halo grows and fades in a slow rhythm — draws the eye without distracting from content elsewhere on the page. Suggested use: always-on idle state.
Option 2
Breathe
Orb scales 1.0 to 1.12 at a 5.0 second cycle
Calmer than the halo pulse. Whole orb expands and contracts. Gentler rhythm — reads as "alive but resting." Suggested use: alternative idle state if pulse feels too active.
Option 3
Rotating Ring
10 second rotation with two accent particles
Most attention-grabbing. Outer ring rotates with two glowing particles orbiting. Reads as "actively working." Suggested use: "Beon is processing" state — when an Ask Beon query is in flight.
Option 4
State Morph
Auto-cycles default ↔ active in a 10 second loop
Demonstrates the transition between default and active states. In production this would only fire on hover or interaction, not as a continuous loop. Suggested use: hover transition between idle and engaged.

Layered usage recommendation

Idle (always-on): Option 1 (Halo Pulse) at the front-end speed. Option 2 (Breathe) is an alternative if pulse feels too busy.

Hover / engaging: Option 4 (State Morph) but triggered by cursor, not on a loop.

Active processing: Option 3 (Rotating Ring) — reserved for "Beon is generating a response." Visible motion communicates work being done.

Avoid: stacking all four at once. Pulse plus breathe plus ring is visually busy and competes with surrounding content.
